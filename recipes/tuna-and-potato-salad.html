Tuna and Potato Salad

Description

This is a family recipe for tuna and potato salad. I have no idea if it's commonly eaten in France or if it's just a thing we ate in my family. But I know that I grew up eating it.

It's very easy to prepare, and in my opinion, it's quite tasty. It's a great summer dish, but honestly, it can be eaten any time of year. I hope you enjoy it as much as I do!

Serves 4 very hungry people

Ingredients

  • 8 russet potatoes
  • 2 cans chunk light tuna, drained
  • Half a medium onion, cut into thin slices, which are then quartered
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/2 cup vegetable or olive oil
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Scrub russets and place into large pot. Add enough water to cover the potatoes, salt the water, and boil until a kitchen knife inserts easily into potatoes. Add more water during boiling as needed.
  2. While potatoes are cooking, combine oil, vinegar, salt, and pepper in large bowl to make vinaigrette.
  3. Add onions and tuna to the vinaigrette.
  4. When potatoes are done cooking, drain them. At this point, you can either peel them or leave the skin on.
  5. Cut cooked potatoes into slices and add them to bowl containing other ingredients.
  6. Add chopped parsley to bowl and mix thoroughly to combine.
  7. Add more salt, pepper, oil, and vinegar as needed.
  8. Serve immediately or let cool. Can be stored in the refrigerator for up to five days.
