Chap Chae

Description

This is a simple but delicious Korean noodle dish. It's vegetarian as-is, but if you're so inclined to add the optional chicken, you won't be sorry.

Serves 4 people

Ingredients

  • 2 oz. cellophane noodles, soaked in 6 cups of water for 30 minutes, drained
  • 6 oz. dried Chinese black mushrooms, soaked in 1 cup of hot water for 20 minutes, sliced
  • 1/3 lb. washed spinach leaves, dropped in boiling water for 2 minutes, drained, squeezed
  • 1 carrot, peeled, julienned
  • 3 medium-sized button mushrooms, finely sliced
  • 2 bock choy leaves, discard tender part, julienne the core only
  • 4 scallions cut into 2-and-a-half-inch secions, quarter each section
  • Optional: 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded or cut into thin slices
  • 4 tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp. sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled, minced
  • 1 tbsp. soy sauce
  • 1 tsp. sugar
  • 1/2 tsp. salt

Steps

  1. Combine all vegetable ingredients (and chicken, if desired), minus the garlic, in a bowl.
  2. Heat vegetable oil and sesame oil in large pan over medium-high flame. Add garlic, stir fry for about 10 seconds.
  3. Add contents of the bowl to the pan, stir fry for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Turn heat to low. Add drained noodles, soy sauce, sugar, and salt. Stir well and cook 2-3 minutes. Serve immediately.
